2. Stayfilm Case Analysis

Guided analysis of the assigned case questions. Use these visual aids to facilitate the discussion.

Q1 Strengths vs. Required Changes

Strengths (The Digital Advantage)

  • Automated Video Creation: Proprietary AI technology lowers barriers for users.
  • Scalability: Cloud-based structure allows global reach without physical assets.
  • Partnerships: Collaboration with Facebook (Internet.org) provided massive initial user base.
  • Asset Light: Low fixed costs compared to traditional production studios.

Changes Needed for Globalization

  • Monetization Shift: Move from B2C (Ads/Freemium) to B2B (Corporate SaaS) for stable revenue.
  • Platform Independence: Reduce over-reliance on Facebook's ecosystem.
  • Language & Culture: Interface and AI themes must adapt to non-Portuguese contexts.

Q2 Why Move the HQ? (Institutional Voids)

Stayfilm's decision in 2017 was driven by the "Push" of Brazil's adverse environment and the "Pull" of global strategic needs.

Push Factors (Brazil Context)

"Custo Brasil": Complex tax system and bureaucracy stifle speed.
Capital Void: Lack of Venture Capital for Series A/B scaling.
Economic Recession: 2015-2016 crisis reduced local B2B demand.
Talent Gap: Shortage of specialized AI/Tech developers.

Pull Factors (Global Strategy)

Hard Currency: Revenue in USD/EUR protects against BRL volatility.
Innovation Hubs: Access to mature ecosystems (e.g., AI in Toronto).
Global Mindset: Amazon mentors challenged them to "think big."
Credibility: A foreign HQ signals stability to global clients.

Q3 Strategic Evaluation of HQ Locations

Comparative analysis of London, Miami, and Toronto based on key strategic drivers for a digital scale-up.

Toronto

The Tech Choice: High AI talent, strong gov support, stability. Challenge: Cold, cultural distance.

Miami

The Cultural Choice: Gateway to LatAm, high cultural fit, tax friendly. Challenge: Less "Deep Tech" talent.

London

The Creative Choice: Global media hub, access to Europe. Challenge: High cost, Brexit risk.

Topic: CAGE Distance Framework

Distance is not just miles. Use this chart to compare the "Distance" from Brazil.

Political Risk & Civil Society

Institutional Voids (Meyer & Thein)

Explain that "voids" are absences of market intermediaries (regulatory systems, reliable data, contract enforcement). Stayfilm left Brazil to escape these voids.

Teaching Tip: Ask students "What is a 'void' you encounter in Colombia?" (e.g., slow justice system).

Civil Society Interactions (Bruijn)

NGOs and Civil Society can be partners or adversaries. For a data company like Stayfilm, privacy advocates are key stakeholders.

Key concept: Legitimacy. Internationalizing requires gaining legitimacy from local stakeholders.

5. Student Assessment Activities (2%)

Interactive components to apply theory and grade progress.

GROUP WORK (1%)

The Headquarters Pitch

Scenario: You are external consultants hired by the Almeida brothers. Your team is assigned ONE city (Miami, Toronto, or London).

Task: Prepare a 3-minute pitch answering: Why is this city the best fit despite its risks?

  • Identify 1 Key Opportunity (Pull Factor).
  • Identify 1 Key Distance Challenge (CAGE) and how to mitigate it.
  • Use data (e.g., tax rates, talent stats).

Grading Rubric

Application of CAGE/PESTLE 40%
Use of Evidence/Data 30%
Persuasion & Clarity 30%
INDIVIDUAL (1%)

The "Risk Memo"

Scenario: Write a short email (150 words) to the CEO.

Task: Based on the city your group pitched, identify the single greatest Political or Institutional Risk (e.g., Visa changes, Data regulation, Trade war). Recommend one specific step to manage it.

Grading Rubric

Identification of Specific Risk 50%
Feasibility of Mitigation 30%
Professionalism & Brevity 20%
